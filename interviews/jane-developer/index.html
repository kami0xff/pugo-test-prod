Building the Future of Open Source

A conversation with Jane Developer about her journey in tech and the future of open source software.

December 20, 2025 45 min

Jane Developer

Lead Maintainer, OpenProject

Q: How did you first get into open source development?

I started contributing to open source during college. I was working on a project and found a bug in a library I was using. Instead of working around it, I decided to fix it and submit a pull request. That first contribution was terrifying but also incredibly rewarding.

Q: What advice would you give to someone starting their open source journey?

Start small. Don’t feel like you need to write the next big framework. Documentation improvements, bug fixes, and helping answer questions are all valuable contributions. The community is generally very welcoming to newcomers.

Q: What’s the biggest challenge facing open source today?

Sustainability is the big one. So many critical projects are maintained by just a handful of people, often in their spare time. We need better funding models and corporate support to keep these projects healthy.

Q: What’s next for you and OpenProject?

We’re working on some exciting features around AI-assisted development. I can’t share too much yet, but I think it’s going to change how people interact with our tools. Stay tuned!

Q: Any final thoughts for our readers?

Just this: the best time to start contributing to open source is now. Find a project you care about, join their community, and don’t be afraid to ask questions. We were all beginners once.
